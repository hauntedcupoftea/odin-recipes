Paneer Cheela

Description

The Paneer Cheela is one of my favorites. It was introduced to me by my cousin when I first started going to the gym and trying to lose weight. It's essentially a form of Roti or Tortilla, except it's made with chickpea batter (known as 'Besan' or Gram Flour). This marvelous flour is perfect for Vegetarians looking to start gyming, as it contains not a lot carbohydrates while giving you an immense 22g of protein per 100 grams.

Paneer is the real star of the show, however. Every vegetarian at some point has had a phase where they've been obsessed with paneer. While having a higher fat content than Besan, paneer is still great. Tofu remains a healthier alternative for those with dietary restrictions or a fear of milk, and this recipe can be modified to subtitute tofu for paneeer to experience that too.

Steps to Prepare

  1. Mix the besan cheela batter again and take a ladleful of batter.
  2. Pour the batter on a hot flat pan.
  3. Spread gently making sure the chilla is slightly thick.
  4. Spread a tsp of oil around the corners.
  5. Flip gently without breaking the chilla.
  6. Press gently, making sure the chilla is cooked from both sides
  7. Spread 2 tbsp of prepared stuffing on half of the chilla.
  8. fold half and roast slightly crisp.
  9. finally, enjoy paneer chilla with green chutney and tamarind chutney.
